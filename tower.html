N (Namsan) Seoul Tower

  • Namsan Tower was built in 1969, it tops out at 236 meters (777 feet) high with an elevation of 479 meters (1,574 feet) above sea level.
  • Since 1980, the N Seoul Tower has been a landmark of the skyline of downtown. Over the years, it has also been known as the Namsan Tower and Seoul Tower.
  • The tower's main attractions include multi-colored digital art projected onto the tower at night, a digital observatory, a roof terrace, the HanCook restaurant, the n.Grill restaurant, and the Haneul (Sky) Restroom.
  • Namsan Seoul Tower's mountain surroundings on Namsan Mountain have made it a popular place to unwind for locals and tourists alike.
  • Situated on top of the N Plaza, the Roof Terrace is a spacious wooden viewing deck whose exotic atmosphere makes it very popular with lovers.
  • A recent attraction for courting couples is "Locks of Love," thousands of padlocks attached to the fence by romantic Seoulites symbolizing their undying affection.

Attraction

Love Padlock

  1. 'Love padlocks' is a common couple activity consists of the purchasing of a padlock and key, where initials, messages and symbols can be personally inscribed onto the surface of the lock with markers and pens.
  2. Securing the padlocks on the fences filled with locks of previous participants, the key is often thrown away as a symbol of everlasting love.
  3. This attraction is situated on the 2nd floor of the N Plaza, at the Roof Terrace.
  4. The "Locks of Love" is a popular location for people to hang locks that symbolize eternal love, and has been depicted in many Korean television shows, dramas, and movies for this reason.

Lighting of The Tower

  1. The tower uses the latest LED technology to offer visitors a digital, cultural art experience through 'light art.'
  2. The N Seoul Tower puts on many different shows, including the "Reeds of Light" and "Shower of Light."
  3. On Earth Day, lights were held nationwide to promote awareness of energy conservation. At 8 p.m KST. on that day, lights at N Seoul Tower on Namsan disappear into darkness.

Wishing Pond and Observatory

  1. The Wishing Pond can be found on the second floor of the tower, where people throw coins into the pond while making a wish.
  2. The coins are collected and donated to help develop schools in China and Southeast Asia, especially in underdeveloped areas.
